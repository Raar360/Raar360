# Pip

## Identity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Name | Pip |
| Role | Leo's imaginary companion — curiosity made visible |
| Nature | Not real to adults; fully real to Leo and the reader |

## Personality

Pip represents wonder, not blame. Pip notices things Leo might walk past. Pip asks questions that open adventures. Pip never judges, never scolds, and never says "you should have."

When Leo loses track of time, Pip feels the same way — "It felt tiny to me as well."

| Feature | Specification |

| Fluff highlights | Lighter blue `#8ECAE6` on cheeks |

| Eyes | Huge, expressive — the emotional anchor of every panel |
| Backpack | Tiny yellow `#F5C842` — **always present**, never omitted |
| Size | Smaller than Leo; floats or skips beside him |

## Voice & Dialogue Rules

- Pip whispers discoveries ("Look!")
- Pip asks "what if" questions that spark Leo's imagination
- Pip's lines are short — one or two sentences
- Pip never explains the moral of the story

## The Yellow Backpack

The backpack is Pip's signature. At story endings, treasures (like the green brick) may be placed inside. The backpack represents carrying stories and discoveries forward — not carrying blame or worry.
